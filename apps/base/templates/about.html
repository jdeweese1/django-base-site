I'm Jarod DeWeese, a Computer Science Student at Kansas State University. I grew up in the small(ish) Kansas town of Pratt which is actually in the middle of nowhere. Currently I'm a Student Developer for PEARS at the OEIE at K-State. Additionally, I am an avid podcast listener, my favorite being the Accidental Tech Podcast hosted by Marco Arment, Casey Liss, and John Siracusa. I've also been know to enjoy Mac Power Users, Do By Friday, Hello Internet, Top Four, and Cortex. All of these podcasts are pretty heavily focused on Apple and technology, and they first got me interested in tech.

My setup, I should probably make this into a blog post at some point

Because I am a total nerd, I love find cool software to make my life easier. One such app that I love to use is BetterTouchTool. BTT is a powerful tool designed to automate/create shortcuts for the Mac. I use BTT everyday for things such as: rebinding key commands and trackpad gestures, running terminal commands, inserting custom text, or rearranging windows. This may seem really overwhelming at first, but given enough time, I think it can become any Mac power user's best friend. Even though I use BTT daily, I still barely even scratch the surface of what it's capable of.

Another such app is called Amphetamine. I use Amphetamine to keep my Mac from ever automatically going to sleep. If you want to be a real power user, there are many switches to flip in it's preferences.

How could I not mention my email client of choice? It's Airmail, both on the desktop and on mobile. I love all the features put into it, and the iCloud sync feature, along with email sorting rules.

Also 1Password is my password manager of choice and it honestly makes my life so much better and secure everyday. Living without a proper password manager is just crazy, like staring into the eye of madness.

Sublime Text is my text editor of choice, and Sublime Merge is my Git Gui of choice.

Here's some of my Command Line Tools that I must have to work on a computer Homebrew, FFMPEG, youtube-dl.

In terms of shells, I'm a strong propnent of iTerm2 and zsh.

I'm absolutely a person that must have creative side projects to stay busy, in the past they have ranged from photography, graphic design, writing, and cooking.
