I'm Jarod DeWeese, a Computer Science Student at Kansas State University. I'm also a Student Developer for PEARS at the OEIE at K-State. Additionally, I am an avid podcast listener, my favorite being the Accidental Tech Podcast hosted by Marco Arment, Casey Liss, and John Siracusa. I've also been know to enjoy Mac Power Users, Do By Friday, Hello Internet, and Cortex.

Because I am a total nerd, I love find cool software to make my life easier. One such app that I love to use is BetterTouchTool. BTT is a powerful tool designed to automate/create shortcuts for the Mac. I use BTT everyday for things such as: rebinding key commands and trackpad gestures, running terminal commands, inserting custom text, or rearranging windows. Although I use BTT regularly, I barely even scratch the surface of what it's capable of.

Another such app is called Amphetamine. I use Amphetamine to keep my Mac from ever automatically going to sleep. If you want to be a real power user, there are many switches to flip in it's preferences.

How could I not mention my email client of choice? It's Airmail, both on the desktop and on mobile. I love all the features put into it, and the iCloud sync feature, along with email sorting rules.

In my free time I enjoy running, baking, reading, programming and writing prose.

My favorite programming languages right now are C# and Python, but I also have experience in Java.
